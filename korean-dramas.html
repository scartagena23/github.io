About Korean Dramas

Korean dramas are more popularly knwon as K-dramas. K-dramas, are television series in the Korean language, made in South Korea. Watching K-dramas is like living another life that is full of charming characters and different plot twists. You will get thriller, comedy, suspense, charming characters, some superficial characters, and lots of Korean food and fashion in one drama. They have grown in popularity due to their widespread availability via streaming services which often offer subtitles in multiple languages. Generally, k-dramas don’t have more than 16 to 30 episodes. Most of them have only 16 episodes. This unique characteristic made dramas show more stories in less time.

You may be asking yourself why to I like k-dramas? For me korean dramas successfully create emotional connections with viewers. Characters are developed and brought through trials and tribulations in such a way that the audience relates to the characters and feels the same emotions. Being able to experience something new is a given with K-dramas, what with the total immersion in Korean culture. We can pick up on some of the cultural nuances embedded into the dramas, like bowing and the different ways of respectfully addressing others. K-dramas also offer insight into key societal issues like bullying and intense pressure regarding education and appearance.You probably have heard about Squid Games, well that is a k-drama. The series revolves around a contest where 456 players, all of whom are in deep financial debt, risk their lives to play a series of deadly children's games for the chance to win a ₩45.6 billion prize. The title of the series draws from a similarly named Korean children's game.

Here is a list of my top 5 K-dramas:

  1. Crash Landing on You
  2. Goblin (Guardian: The Lonely and Great God)
  3. Vincenzo
  4. The King: Eternal Monarch
  5. It's Okay to Not Be Okay
